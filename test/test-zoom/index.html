Teens don't really spend much time keeping a treatment diary, data reporting, planning for medical emergencies, etc.

In fact, they don't spend any. Even if they have a disease.

And that's one of the worst parts about having chronic disease like hemophilia; the paperwork.

—keeping treatment logs, tracking medication quantities, planning for medical emergencies, etc.—

It's hard for adults to keep up with.

If you're a kid with hemophilia, getting a cut, bruise or joint bleed could turn deadly. Witch means a trip to the emergency room, panic and, of course, paperwork.

Essentially, your day is ruined.

AmerisourceBergen's patent-pending myCubixx® goes a long way to make sure that days and childhoods aren't ruined.

An in–home medication storage and inventory management system, the RFID–enabled technology in myCubixx helps patients manage their disease state by maintaining adherence and recording and reporting vital data.

Here's how it works:

  • After an event, the patient inputs their details and pain rating on the myCubixx touch–pad tablet.
  • myCubixx records that information and provides access to product.
  • myCubixx then registers how much product is removed and can instantly notify healthcare providers and caregivers.

These simple steps cut down on trips to the emergency room, manual data reporting and panic.

All of which allows teens to focus on being teens, instead of managing their disease.
