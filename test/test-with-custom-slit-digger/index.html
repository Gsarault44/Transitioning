Where can you go without having to make an appointment to speak with a healthcare professional about medication you’re taking, get help managing your diabetes or get a flu shot?

The community pharmacy.

Just walk right up to the counter and speak with the pharmacist.

Who, most likely, is also the owner.

It’s that ability to just walk right in that makes community pharmacies such a vital part of any neighborhood and a critical link in the healthcare system.

Through the Good Neighbor Pharmacy brand, AmerisourceBergen helps around 3,200 independent, local pharmacies across the country build their businesses with resources like inventory management, business consulting services and continuing education.

Those services are backed up by a robust distribution network. Sourcing more than 50,000 different items from over 1,500 manufactures through 26 large scale distribution centers across the United States, AmerisourceBergen makes sure that no matter where a Good Neighbor Pharmacy is located—a small town way out on the prairie or on a busy city street corner‐it is always well stocked.

And with the upcoming challenges facing community pharmacies brought on by the Affordable Healthcare Act and CMS Star Measures rating system, the partnership with AmerisourceBergen will only continue to grow and strengthen.

All of which—the horsepower and the brainpower—will allow Good Neighbor pharmacists/owners to keep their business thriving and healthy.

Because the healthier their business is, the healthier their patients, and community, will be.

Managing growth, addressing change and planning for the future takes past experiences as well as innovative, foward thinking. It takes a global healthcare solutions leader dedicated to enhancing patient care through end-to-end solutions for manufacturers, pharmacies and providers. Its takes hindsight and foresight. A good chunk of a pharmacist’s day is spent pulling product, doing inventory and restocking.

There had to be an easier way

Neil Herson, President of ASD Healthcare, a division of AmerisourceBergen, was holding that easier way in his hand.

And that easier way was a bottle of water.

Getting ready to checkout of his hotel, Neil reached into his room’s mini-fridge to grab a bottle of water. By the time he got to the lobby, the bottle of water was already on his bill.

He quickly deduced that the mini-fridge used RFID tags to ascertain which items he had taken out and relayed it to the hotel’s billing system.

Which seemed like a lot of technology just for a bottle of water.

But thinking about it, the RFID tags were probably a lot more efficient than having someone manually check every mini-fridge, everyday, to do inventory and restock.

And if it worked for hotels, then maybe it could work for pharmacies.

Not only that, maybe it could even work for patients.

Especially teen patients.

Because teens don’t really think much about restocking medicine.

Or keeping a disease diary, data reporting, planning for medical emergencies, contacting their doctor or parents if they do have an emergency ‐ you get the idea

Even if they have a disease.

Like hemophilia, for instance.

Because for the 17,000 people in the U.S. who have hemophilia, that’s one of the most taxing aspects about the disease; all the work that goes into managing it.

Not to mention the fact that getting a cut, bruise or scrape could turn deadly.

Which means a trip to the emergency room, contacting doctors, contacting family members and the panic that ensues for all involved.

It’s a lot to manage and keep track of.

Hence, the creation of MyCubixx.

An in-home medication storage and delivery device, MyCubixx utilizes RFID enabled technology to help patients manage their disease state by handling diagnosis, dosing, dispensing, data reporting, contacting doctors, parents and caregivers.

Here's how it works:

  • After an event, the patient inputs their bleed details, target joint, and pain rating on the myCubixx touch‐pad tablet.
  • MyCubixx records that information and provides access to product as well as dosage instruction.
  • MyCubixx then records how much product is taken and instantly notifies the patient’s doctor, family members and pharmacy so that new medicine can be pulled and the unit automatically restocked.

But most importantly, for parents and teens, MyCubixx goes a long way to helping manage the panic associated with an event.

And with the panic managed, teens can focus on just being teens instead of a disease.

Because when you combine a little foresight and understanding with a company that can handle 5,000 individual, specialty pharmaceutical prescriptions a day with 99.7% accuracy, amazing things are possible.

And having an RFID enabled bottle of water on hand doesn’t hurt either.

Medication non-adherence: Costs the healthcare system around $290 billion a year1 Results in an estimated 125,000 deaths annually.2 50 percent of medications for chronic disease are not taken as prescribed.3

Medication non‐adherence is one of the biggest challenges facing healthcare systems and patients today.

Utilizing a combination of innovative technologies and personal, one‐on‐one interactions, AmerisourceBergen not only helps patients get access to critical medications, but stay on them as well.

Nowhere is this challenge more prevalent than in long term care facilities.

Not only are you dealing with multiple patients with multiple regimens, but those regimens tend to be fairly complex.

Of all age groups, the elderly take the highest numbers of medications.4 More than 76% of Americans over 60 are on two medications. And of those, 37% are on five or more medications.5

One of the major reasons this particular patient population stops taking their medications is because they don’t understand how or when to take them. And the more medications they’re on, the more confusing it gets.

The technology in AmerisourceBergen’s FastPak EXP helps address those problems.

A high volume packaging system that can consolidate multiple medications into individual packages, the FastPak EXP is capable of filling up to 65 customized pouches per minute.

Each package is clearly‐labeled with easy‐to‐understand information for the patient including; patient name, time of dosage, individual drug description and dose quantity.

The ability to deliver custom solutions at high speed allows a long term care facility's pharmacy to run more efficiently and eliminate errors. Which results in superior care and better outcomes for their patients.

On the personal, individual side there is well, actually, a person.

Her name is Martha.

She is one of the thousands of patient counselors, working across over 100 patient support programs, from AmerisourceBergen’s Lash Group’s patient assistance programs that help patients get on—and stay on—various therapies.

So, after a patient has been diagnosed with something terrible, like kidney cancer.

And they're sitting in their house.

Wondering if they’ll see next Christmas.

Wondering if the oral chemo they’ve been prescribed will even work.

Wondering if they should even bother taking it.

That’s when Martha calls.

“Martha calls me every few weeks and I can call her anytime. She always understands and never rushes me. She explained how the drug works and how to combat side effects. We go over my tests. She let me know that some Stage Four patients have been on [Oral Chemotherapy] for more than six years. She helps me frame questions to ask my oncologist about or tests I could suggest to help monitor me.”

She brought light into my dark tunnel.

She empowered me.

Adherence isn’t just about taking pills. It’s about empowerment.

About living a better life.

And it’s that combination between technology like FastPak EXP and people like Martha that drive that empowerment and drive AmerisourceBergen.
